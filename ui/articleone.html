Departures.

If you were a branch,

I would be this leaf,
clinging loosely to one of your nodes.

Holding on, still.
Against the sudden gust and the kiss of gravity.

Trying not to feed my vulnerability,
so as to sustain our little connection.

But it was the season of fall.

So, I had to let go,
for you to survive
the winters.

Worst is getting stuck in a traffic jam
on a highway of confusion,
When your explanations have to stand in a queue 
waiting for clearance.

And amidst these honks full of hate,
And air so thick,
I’d rather let my patience out.

There’s no absolute need for hunting reasons to fit an empty apology.
No meaning could ever be found in rupturing your own organics, 
out of an unexplainable itch for saving the day.

Much has already been dissolved in so less of it.

I see a saturated solution, 
still trying to stir itself,
and settle.

Half an hour to 1200AM.

And there I was, lying on my bed, staring at the top and trying to catch with the pace of one of the revolving blades of my ceiling fan.

Half an hour to 1200AM, I confirmed as I made an eye contact with my phone’s screen. It would be an end of a tiresome day and I still don’t blame the practice sessions for the guard of honour which was to be performed on the coming Independence day. It wasn’t that. Neither it was about tomorrow.

It was just a 10 digit number on a screen ready to be dialled. And, exactly half an hour from now it was going to lose its meaning.

A year back, the same day wasn’t the same. A Birthday, of someone somewhere far away was being missed. And, with the whole three months of break and rehabilitation, I was still new to the process of keeping up with everyday.

It was uneasy, but maybe I could trust the different versions of me trying to fit a justification in a void of dilemma and confusion, reminding me of these happy facebook posts that I’ve been reading on her timeline throughout the day.

We weren’t friends on facebook. But maybe, that was something different altogether.

“What if I just skip it?”

Maybe, it was for the best. And this is how the story should’ve ended.
But on being carefully dissected, things didn’t seem to be fair this way. So, I finally picked up the phone and dialled. A small ring was followed by a mechanised voice telling me that the number was busy. I tried it again, and could still hear a ring just before the message.

My number was blocked.

So, I took a deep breath, and started arranging my bed to sleep. I knew it would be a lot more difficult now. But, I was finally through with my state of confusion.
I rested my head on the pillow and wrapped myself with a newly dry cleaned blanket, tried to shut my eyes thinking that would help making things appear bit more darker than the room itself. I wasn’t thinking anymore but I was clearly full of questions that would never seek their answers.
But it was then when I felt my phone vibrating. It was ringing. The number wasn’t saved but I clearly remembered the order to put zeros and nines and ones and twos that would finally end up making her contact number .
It was 1158 at night.So, I immediately picked it up.

There was a small pause in the beginning.
But, it wasn’t late enough to wish her a “Happy Birthday.”.

 
Yugal Gautam | 2016 THE LITERARY MESH
